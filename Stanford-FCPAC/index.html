About

FCPAC (Foreign Corrupt Practices Act Clearinghouse) is both a FCPA litigation database with analytics and a repository of court documents.

Leslie A. Gordon (MA Ã­98), in an article in the Stanford Lawyer Magazine, said in 2010: "Empirical research is not without challenges or flaws. Despite sophisticated statistical methods, the resulting inferences are only as good as the data on which researchers rely."

The challenge is then to improve and optimize the data gathering methodologies. This is where we concentrate our resources. We also allow full text search on top of the database. Most website allow you to either search the structure data or the text of the documents. In this website, using the Advanced Search, you can search the fields and the content of the documents simultaneously.

This database presents in various ways FCPA cases. We believe that one of our mayor contributions is grouping this data by FCPA Matters, which is constitute by those legal proceedings that arise out of a common bribery scheme or group of schemes involving bribery.

As of today, the FCPA has led to more than 300 SEC, DOJ and other enforcement agencies actions involving more than 90 countries. We have comprised all of these action or legal proceedings in 110 FCPA Matters.
